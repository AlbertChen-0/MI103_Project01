What is Galaxy-Eyes' Specialty?

The most standout feature of Galaxy-Eyes is probably their abundant boss monsters, which also have high attack points. Their ability to perform one-turn kills going second is one of the best.

The second specialty would be the ability to rip monsters out from opponents’ extra decks, sometimes rendering the opponents' deck completely unusable. If the opponent has a Number Monster in their extra deck, the Galaxy-Eyes player could also steal it and summon it to their side of the field.

Key Cards
Combo Starter

Starter is what Galaxy-Eyes deck lacks the most, but it is still enough to be count as a fairly consistent deck. Generally, players would want to start with Galaxy Hundred in their hand, but if there isn’t, player can normal summon Galaxy Wizard to use it to search Galaxy Hundred directly into their hand. Foolish Burial having a similar effect of sending a card to the graveyard makes it a great substitute for Galaxy Hundred. As the deck i now, player will not usually use Galaxy Soldier as a combo starter, but it is still a option.

Combo Extender

Extenders is what Galaxy-Eyes deck has access to the most, there are so much of them that I need to cut them down to four cards to focus on. Photon Jumper is the target that Galaxy Hundred and Foolish Burial will be send to the graveyard. With its effect, it can search Galaxy Trance into the players hand and summon Galaxy Photon Dragon with it with the cost of 2000 life points. At this point, player would want to use Galaxy Photon Dragon’s effect to search for either Photon Emperor or Photon Soldier, depending on which the player is missing.

Turn 1 End Board

Galaxy-Eyes’ first turn end board is decently powerful. With Starliege Photon Dragon and Galaxy Photon Dragon on the field, player’s light monsters with attack above 2000 all cannot be targeted or destroyed by opponents effect. Galaxy-Eyes end board usually have two negates. One which is also the priority, is No.90 Galaxy-Eyes Photon Lord. Not only can it serve as a disruption, it can also replenish resource on the opponents turn and search up whatever galaxy or photon cards the player needs at the moment. The second negate is one of the most commonly seen disruption monster in the game, being No.38 Hope Harbinger Dragon Titanic Galaxy, its powerful effect of negating and stealing opponents spell cards makesvit a great asset.

Game Ender

Galaxy-Eyes’ most renown characteristic is their ability to end the game with a single strike. With the combination of either No.62 Galaxy-Eyes Prime Photon Dragon or CNo.62 Neo Galaxy-Eyes Prime Photon Dragon and Galaxy-Eyes Afterglow Dragon, The two Number Monsters could easily obtain attack points over 10000. Though the regular No.62 could obtain more attack power, the Chaos version of it has immunity to all opponents monster effect, while also could attack opponents monsters three times in a turn. Because of this, there is no definitive which to use over the other, but use each of them according to the situation.
